Condition Scale Helper

  1. Poor (P): The record has significant wear, deep scratches, and may skip or have substantial surface noise. The sleeve is heavily damaged, with tears, discoloration, and possible writing on it.
  2. Fair (F): The record shows noticeable signs of wear, with surface scratches and audible surface noise during playback. The sleeve has significant wear and may have writing, split seams, or tape.
  3. Good (G): The record has been played frequently, with less luster, noticeable scratches, and may have some surface noise, but no major distortion. The sleeve shows normal wear and tear but is intact.
  4. Very Good (VG): The record has been handled and played but is well cared for. There may be light scratches that don't affect playback. The sleeve may have slight wear or creasing, but overall it's in good shape.
  5. Near Mint (NM): The record has been played but shows no signs of deterioration in sound quality. There may be very minor signs of wear. The sleeve is close to perfect with only minor signs of handling.
  6. Mint (M): The record is in perfect condition. It's likely never been played. The sleeve is pristine, with no creases, ring wear, or other signs of handling.
